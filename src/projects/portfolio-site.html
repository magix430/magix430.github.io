Portfolio Site

Spring 2019 - Welcome to my portfolio site!



What inspired your site design?

I have nearly 2500 hours in Hexcells, a minesweeper game set in hexagons. Many of its visual elements inspired my website design: the color choice, thin font styling, and emphasis on minimalism.

I believe strongly in simple, intuitive, and interactive user interfaces.

Why from scratch?

My experience with Wix and SquareSpace was simple and short - I found the interface stifling.

The templates were certainly cool and organized. But I wanted a little more - I wanted to make something that would make people say "Wow, I've never seen that before."

My website had humble beginnings - I wanted it to be easy to make, easy for the eye, and filled with my latest obsession: hexagons.

The industrial grey background, sharp-cut corners, and grid-based format of each page gave the sense of organization and control.

I had originally wanted to frame each relevant image in a hexagon, but quickly realized that it would be 1) difficult to code and 2) hard to frame certain things.

I switched to using rectangles, but realized that my page looked generic. Sure, everything was in the right place, but it felt too "empty", expression-wise.

"I am a gamer, composer, and designer."

[Hexcells]: Gameplay

One topic that came up frequently when receiving feedback was how the landing page expressed that "I am a gamer, composer, and designer."

There was little on the website besides a few projects and the statement itself that expressed that I was the gamer I said I was.

My Steam profile showed that I loved games more than my site did, but how would I carry it over?

It was back to the drawing board. Once creative and personal expression entered the equation, I knew that building the website would no longer be easy.

I wanted to make my website a game by itself - you'd win by visiting the page and having fun, you'd lose by... not visiting it, I suppose :)

The first challenge came from figuring out what I had to work with.

I had some prior experience working in webpages (HTML/CSS) but very little coding experience in terms of Javascript, React, Gatsby, ...etc.

I looked through several online assets and found several useful tools like JSFiddle and CodePen.

My browsers quickly filled with tabs of these two services.

They were live-development code editors, meaning that you could see what you were making - and many people had many projects to share.

The main challenge was that the assets I desired - hexagons, minimaps, hover-effects - often had outdated or defunct code.

I either had to search harder or learn a bit of code.
